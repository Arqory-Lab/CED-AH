## Plain Language Summary

Before you contribute, here's what this agreement does and doesn't do in plain language.

|     | What This Agreement Does                                                                                                             |
| --- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| ✅   | **Lets us distribute your contribution** under the project's open source licenses (Apache 2.0 for code, CC BY 4.0 for documentation) |
| ✅   | **Lets us include your contribution** in commercially licensed versions of the project                                               |
| ✅   | **Preserves your copyright** - you keep full ownership of everything you write                                                       |
| ✅   | **Grants you all rights** to use your contribution however you want, independently of this project                                   |
| ✅   | **Binds us to always keep your contribution available** under the project's current open source license                              |

| | What This Agreement Does NOT Do |
|---|---|
| ❌ | Transfer your copyright to us |
| ❌ | Prevent you from using your own contribution elsewhere |
| ❌ | Give us the right to remove your contribution from the open-source version |
| ❌ | Require you to provide support for your contribution |
| ❌ | Create any employment or contractor relationship |

**Why we need a CLA:** The CED-AH project is maintained by a nonprofit research institute with a related commercial entity.  To sustain open research through commercial deployment, we need the ability to offer the project under both open-source and commercial licenses.  Without a CLA, contributor code can only be distributed under the original open-source license — which would prevent the dual-licensing model that funds our open research.

**Our commitment to you:** As a condition of this agreement, we commit to always making your contribution available under the project's current open-source license (Apache 2.0 for code, CC BY 4.0 for documentation).  Your work will always remain open, even if it is also included in commercial offerings.
