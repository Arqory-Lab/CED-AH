## Strength Assessment

**Rating:** <!-- Strong | Moderate | Suggestive -->

<!--
  Assessment criteria:
  
  STRONG — The evidence directly and rigorously demonstrates the claim.
  Methodology is sound, sample sizes are adequate, conclusions follow
  from the data, and the work has survived peer review or formal scrutiny.
  For philosophical evidence: the argument is logically valid and the
  premises are well-established.
  
  MODERATE — The evidence is consistent with the claim and comes from
  a credible source, but has limitations: small sample size, indirect
  measurement, limited generalizability, contested methodology, or the
  connection to the claim requires interpretive steps.
  
  SUGGESTIVE — The evidence points in the direction of the claim but is
  preliminary, indirect, or from a source that has not been fully vetted.
  Useful for establishing plausibility and identifying research directions,
  but insufficient to bear significant argumentative weight on its own.
