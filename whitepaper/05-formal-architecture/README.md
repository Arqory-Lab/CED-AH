# Part 5: The Formal Architecture

## Summary

This part lays out the CED framework's complete logical structure, organized by epistemic status so every claim is tagged by how certain it is and what would be required to overturn it. The four-tier architecture moves from the single self-presupposing axiom (Tier 1), through six defended-but-contestable commitments (Tier 2), four bridging principles connecting description to prescription (Tier 3), to five design imperatives derived as theorems (Tier 4). S4 is reframed as a **substitution-amplification continuum** — position determined by degree of active human cognitive engagement, explicitly technology-independent, with a meaning-making criterion distinguishing engagement from interaction. S5 frames the **irreversibility gradient** as a compounding opportunity: early-stage design choices have outsized positive influence, not just outsized risk.

## Architecture At A Glance

| Tier | Contents | Status | To reject, you must... |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | 1 axiom (T1) | Self-presupposing | Argue incoherently |
| **2** | 6 commitments (S1–S6) | Defended positions | Propose a better alternative |
| **3** | 4 bridges (B1–B4) | Connecting claims | Show the bridge premise is false |
| **4** | 5 design imperatives | Derived conclusions | Reject a premise in Tiers 1–3 |

## Key Claims

- T1 (coherence presupposition) is the sole claim that cannot be denied without performative self-contradiction
- S3 (instruments, not agents) and S4 (substitution degrades) bear the most structural weight; S3 cross-references the full agency conjunction defense in Part 4
- S4 reframed as a **substitution-amplification continuum**: position determined by degree of active human cognitive engagement, with continuum position as a *design choice* not a technology property; technology-independence is explicit
- S5 reframed as **compounding opportunity**: early design choices have outsized positive influence; the irreversibility gradient works both ways
- Bridging principle B2 rests on a contestable premise: authority requires moral responsibility, not just reliability — defended via the accountability argument, accuracy objection rebuttal, and time-critical edge case
- All five design imperatives are derived — they are output, not input
- The architecture formalizes the **Instrument Thesis** (Commitment 1), which pairs with the **Accountability Principle** (Commitment 2) to form the **Two Commitments**

## Evidence Base

4 evidence items: 1 philosophical, 1 historical, 1 empirical, 1 mathematical.

Goldman on social epistemics and reliabilism; Meadows on leverage points in complex systems; Simon on bounded rationality and the sciences of the artificial; Godel on incompleteness theorems and formal system limits.
