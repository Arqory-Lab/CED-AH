# Part 4: From "AI" to "Amplified Humanity"

## Summary

This part establishes what computational systems actually are — ontologically — and derives the reframe from "Artificial Intelligence" to "Amplified Humanity." Through analysis of the language problem ("AI" embeds replacement assumptions), the ontological facts (computation is human-dependent, not self-sustaining), and a conjunctive agency test (five conditions, all required, current systems fail all five), the chapter arrives at the AH reframe: same technology, fundamentally different relationship to humans. Engineering precedent demonstrates that the highest-performing human-machine systems are not the most autonomous but the most thoughtfully collaborative — often with *more* automation than replacement-oriented alternatives, but with authority architecture that preserves human decision-making. Adversarial engagement with Dennett's intentional stance and Floridi & Sanders's artificial moral agents strengthens the instrument thesis by addressing the strongest counter-positions directly.

## Key Arguments

- "Artificial Intelligence" embeds replacement and agency assumptions that shape design
- Computation exists entirely in the human-maintained category, not the self-sustaining category
- Agency requires all five conditions met (conjunctive): self-sustaining existence, genuine self-model, goal generation, volition, continuous existence
- The conjunctive scoring captures the *unity* of agency; a disjunctive test would attribute agency to thermostats
- The infant objection is addressed: biological developmental trajectory is not engineered artifact dependency
- Concrete revision triggers specified: operational continuity, novel goal generation, genuine self-reference
- Engineering precedent: comparison table contrasts authority architecture, not automation level
- Historical examples (aviation, surgery, exploration) involve high automation — amplification is not anti-automation
- Adversarial engagement: Dennett's intentional stance and Floridi & Sanders's moral agents addressed directly
- The **Instrument Thesis** (Commitment 1) and the **Accountability Principle** (Commitment 2) are introduced as the Two Commitments
