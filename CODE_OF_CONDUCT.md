# Code of Conduct

**The Arqory Research Institute: CED-AH Project**
**Version 1.0 — February 2026**

---

## Our Standard

CED-AH is a philosophical and technical project built on structured disagreement.  Challenging claims, contesting evidence, and proposing alternatives are not violations of community standards.  They are among the most valuable contributions anyone can make.  This Code of Conduct exists to protect that culture of rigorous engagement, not to suppress it.

The standard is simple: **engage with ideas honestly, treat people with respect, and assume the person you're disagreeing with knows something you don't.**

---

## Expected Conduct

**Argue positions, not people.** "This claim is unsupported because the cited study has methodological flaws X and Y" is excellent engagement.  "Anyone who believes this clearly hasn't read the literature" is not.  The distinction matters: one advances the framework, the other drives away contributors.

**Ground claims in evidence.**  This project has an explicit evidence structure. When you make an assertion, whether supporting or challenging the framework, cite your sources. Unsupported assertions carry less weight here than in most forums.  That applies equally to maintainers and contributors.

**State uncertainty honestly.**  Say "I believe" when you believe.  Say "the evidence shows" when it does. Say "I don't know" when you don't.  Epistemic honesty is a core value of this project, and the community should model it.

**Respect disciplinary differences.**  Contributors come from philosophy, computer science, mathematics, cognitive science, policy, law, and other fields.  Each discipline has its own standards of evidence, argumentation, and rigor.  What counts as a strong argument in formal logic differs from what counts in empirical psychology.  Engage with those differences productively rather than dismissing unfamiliar methodologies.

**Welcome newcomers.**  Not everyone arrives with the same background or vocabulary.  A contributor asking a basic question about coherence theory is a potential collaborator, not an interruption.  Point them to relevant sections of the documentation.  The [glossary](appendices/glossary.md) exists for this reason.

**Keep scope appropriate.**  Issues are for actionable work.  Discussions are for conversation.  Challenges go through the challenge template.  This structure exists to keep the project manageable and to respect everyone's time, including yours.

## Unacceptable Conduct

**Personal attacks and harassment.**  Criticism of ideas is welcome.  Criticism of people including insults, threats, intimidation, doxxing, unwanted sexual attention, or sustained personal hostility is not tolerated under any circumstances.

**Bad faith engagement.**  This includes: repeatedly raising objections that have already been addressed without new evidence; misrepresenting others' positions to make them easier to attack; concern trolling (feigning good faith engagement to disrupt); and deliberate derailing of productive threads.

**Discrimination.**  Exclusion or hostility based on age, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, body size, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

**Intellectual dishonesty.**  Fabricating evidence, misrepresenting sources, plagiarizing others' work, or knowingly citing retracted or discredited research without disclosure.  In a project built on evidence integrity, intellectual dishonesty is a particularly serious violation.

**Spam and self promotion.**  Using project channels primarily to promote unrelated products, services, or projects.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces: GitHub repositories, issues, pull requests, Discussions, and any other communication channels established by the project.  It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

It does not apply to participants' conduct in unrelated contexts.  We govern project spaces, not people's lives.

---

## Enforcement

### Process

1. **Report.**  Contact the project maintainer at - research@arqory.ai with a description of the incident, including links or screenshots where applicable. Reports are reviewed confidentially.

2. **Review.**  The maintainer will review the report, consider context, and determine whether a violation occurred.  If additional information is needed, the maintainer may contact the reporter or other involved parties.

3. **Response.**  Responses are proportional to the violation:

   **Correction** - For first time, minor, or unintentional violations.  A private message explaining what was inappropriate and why, with a request to adjust behavior.  No public action.

   **Warning** - For clear violations or repeated minor violations.  A public or private warning with specific expectations for future conduct.  Continued violation after a warning escalates to the next level.

   **Temporary restriction** - For serious violations or pattern of behavior after warning.  Temporary loss of ability to interact with the project (commenting, submitting PRs, participating in Discussions) for a defined period.

   **Permanent ban** - For severe violations (threats, harassment, sustained bad faith) or repeated violations after temporary restriction.  Permanent removal from all project spaces.

### Principles

**Proportionality.**  The response matches the severity.  A heated comment in an otherwise constructive exchange is not treated the same as targeted harassment.

**Context matters.**  Vigorous intellectual disagreement is expected in this project.  Enforcement distinguishes between passionate argument about ideas (protected) and personal hostility toward people (not protected).  When the line is ambiguous, the maintainer will err on the side of preserving open discourse while addressing conduct that makes participation unwelcoming.

**Transparency within privacy.**  Enforcement actions at the Warning level and above are documented (without identifying the reporter).  The community can see that standards are maintained. Reporter identity is never disclosed without consent.

## A Note on Disagreement

This project makes strong philosophical claims.  Some of those claims challenge widely held positions in AI research, philosophy, and technology development. We expect and need people who disagree with us to engage.

If you think coherence is not the first principle, tell us why.  If you think computational systems can be agents, present the argument.  If you think the five design imperatives are insufficient, propose alternatives.  The [challenge template](.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/challenge-evidence.md) exists specifically to make this kind of engagement structured, productive, and recognized.

The line is not between agreement and disagreement.  It is between good faith and bad faith.  Disagree with rigor and you will find this community welcomes you.  Engage in bad faith and you will find it does not.
